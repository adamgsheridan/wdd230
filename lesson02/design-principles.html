Example 1: Visual Hierarchy

Squarespace

  • Visual Hierarchy is the design principle where things on a webpage that are more important are brighter or more eye catching than other, less important things. This website is a great example of Visual Hierarchy because the brightest parts(such as the get started tabs) are the most important to people who are using the website.

Example 2: Hick's Law

Harvard University

  • Hick's Law is the design principle where a user can make more decisions more quickly due to the amount of decisions that they are required to make and the complexity of the decisions. Harvard's website is a good example of this becasue there are three options to click on. This means that anyone using the website will not have to spend very much time deciding where they are going to go.

Example 3: Fitt's Law

Android

  • Fitt's Law is the design principle where things that are meant to be clicked on such as menus or links should be large enough to see/click on and far enough away from other things so that the user does not click on the wrong thing. The android website does a good job of implementing this design tool because each option in the menu are written on large buttons at the top. This allows for easy access.
